A Look Back At The Semester

Throughout the semester, I have learned new things from each of my various classes.

In English, I learned tools to improve my writing. This was one of the most helpful classes because I will take this information with me for the rest of my life and use it in many facets of my life.

In calculus, I got a refresher on lessons I had previously learned, which will benefit me next semester when I take calculus two.

A class I learned the most new information was intro to microeconomics. This kind of study differed from most things I learned in high school; however, I found it very interesting to see how economics and business correspond. I learned about different markets like monopoly, oligopoly, monopolistic, and perfect competition. They all had differences that made them better in some aspects and atrocious in others.

The intro to computer programming was another brand new class. In high school, I took java and html classes, but I had never seen python before. The class was not only beneficial in teaching me python, but it also taught me some other basic programming lessons that I will take with me.

My last class, Science Gateway Seminar, taught me a wide variety of topics, including AI, autonomous cars, and facial recognition. My knowledge was limited in these topics, and it helped open my eyes to the larger world of technology.
